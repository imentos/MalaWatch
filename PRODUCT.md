# MalaWatch Product Notes

## Core Bet

iPhone is the fastest path to validate the product because it is easy to build, share, screenshot, localize, and test with different audiences. The core ritual should feel good on the phone first: one calm screen, a large tactile bead counter, gentle haptics, and clear progress toward 27, 54, or 108.

Apple Watch is still a strong later extension because the ritual is tactile, glanceable, private, and wearable.

## Audience

- Gen Z and millennial users interested in mindfulness, spiritual routines, yoga,
  meditation, or self-regulation.
- Buddhist and Hindu users who already understand mala / japa practice.
- Users who want a secular ritual counter for breathwork, affirmations, or focus.

## Market Language

Use `Mala` as the main international product word and `Prayer Beads Counter` as
the explanatory subtitle. Avoid leading with `Buddhist` in the brand unless a
specific listing or keyword field needs it.

## MVP iPhone Flow

1. User opens the iPhone app.
2. Screen shows a circular bead progress ring and current count.
3. User taps the large center counter to count one bead.
4. Every bead gives subtle haptic feedback.
5. Reaching the bead goal gives completion haptics and increments rounds.
6. User can reset the current round, switch 27 / 54 / 108, edit label text, and choose a theme.

## Watch Companion Flow

1. User opens the Watch app.
2. User taps or rotates the Digital Crown to count.
3. Watch mirrors the same goal, label, theme, and round count.
4. Complication can later show progress such as `37/108`.

## Design Principles

- Quiet, premium, restrained.
- No heavy religious claims.
- Respectful of traditions without pretending all practices are identical.
- Works fully offline.
- No account in v1.

## Later Ideas

- Watch complication showing `37/108`.
- Mindful Minutes integration.
- Streaks and weekly rhythm.
- Custom haptic styles.
- iCloud sync.
- Theme packs inspired by wood, jade, obsidian, sandalwood, and minimal graphite.
