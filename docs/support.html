Frequently Asked Questions

How do I count beads?

Tap anywhere on the bead wheel to advance one bead, or swipe up to count. When you reach your bead goal (27, 54, or 108), the app completes the round with a pulse of haptic feedback and increments your round counter.

How does the chant guide work?

The chant bar at the top shows the six syllables of Om Mani Padme Hum. The current syllable lights up as you count. In Follow mode, the app speaks each syllable aloud as you advance — keeping your rhythm without looking at the screen. In Silent mode, the syllables are highlighted visually only.

The voice isn't speaking — how do I fix it?

Make sure your iPhone is not in Silent mode (check the physical ring/silent switch on the side). Also ensure the volume is turned up. The chant voice uses your device's built-in text-to-speech, so it respects your system volume and mute settings.

How do I change my bead goal?

Open Settings (the gear icon on the main screen) and tap Bead Goal to choose 27, 54, or 108.

How do I reset my count?

Swipe down on the bead wheel or tap the Reset button in the top-left corner to reset the current round back to bead 1. Your completed rounds are preserved. To reset all rounds, use the Reset option in Settings.

How do I change the bead theme?

Open Settings and tap Bead Style to see all seven themes. Sandalwood is free. The six premium themes (Rosewood, Walnut, Agarwood, Jade, Obsidian, Graphite) require MalaWatch Premium.

What is MalaWatch Premium?

MalaWatch Premium is a one-time purchase that unlocks all six premium bead materials and the Apple Watch companion app. It's yours forever — no subscription.

How do I restore my purchase after reinstalling?

Open Settings in the app and tap Restore Purchase. You must be signed into the same Apple ID that made the original purchase.

Does MalaWatch work on Apple Watch?

Yes — the Apple Watch companion app is included with MalaWatch Premium. Open the MalaWatch app on your Watch to count with a tap or the Digital Crown. Each bead gives a click haptic, and completing a round gives a success pattern.

Does MalaWatch require an internet connection?

No. MalaWatch is fully offline. It never connects to the internet. Your practice data is stored locally on your device only.

Can I use MalaWatch for practices other than Buddhist chanting?

Absolutely. MalaWatch works for any counting practice — Catholic rosary prayers, Hindu japa, affirmations, breathwork, or any personal ritual. You can set a custom label in Settings.

Will my practice data sync across devices?

Not in version 1.0. iCloud sync across devices is planned for a future update. Currently, your count history lives on the device where you practice.

How do I request a refund?

Refund requests are handled by Apple. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com to submit a refund request.
